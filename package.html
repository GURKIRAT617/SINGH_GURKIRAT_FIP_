WILDERNESS NATURE: FEED THE CANADA JAY and CHICKADEE

Canadas new official Bird; the Canada Jay and of course the Chick-a-dee! Touching Wildlife.

Famous birds that will eat seeds from your hand or your hat are the Canada Jay (aka the Gray Jay or the Whisky Jack) and Boreal Chickadee.For the Aficionado Birder check the latest winter birding report for what is being seen and where. 1) Algonquin Logging Museum Trail 2) Spruce Bog Boardwalk Trail3) Mew Lake Old Railway Trail We Provide Free to Use for our guests:- Pro Ice Hiking Crampons and Walking Poles- Park Pass from our Office- Bird seeds- Please never scatter or leave seed behind on the trails

CROSS COUNTRY SKIING and SNOWSHOEING

Tranquil with peacefulness. Algonquin Park has International quality ski trails; track set and skating lane. 48 kilometres of groomed and 23 kilometres of non-groomed cross-country ski trails available in three low-usage trail networks, typically December to early April. Check the latest ski trail conditionsIf you cannot go skiing you can always go IceHiking.

Provided Free to use:- Wax-less cross-country skis, boots and poles.- Waxing station for those that enjoy waxing.- Park Pass from our Office- Pro snowshoes- Park Pass from our Office Serene spots for Snowshoeing:1) Algonquin Outdoor Logging Trail2) Spruce Bog Boardwalk Trail3) Mew Lake Old Railway Trail Go to Mew Lake Campground for starting point.
